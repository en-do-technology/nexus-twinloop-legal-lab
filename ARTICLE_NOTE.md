## Working title

**AiLawyer.world: Why Legal AI Should Start With Boundaries, Not Promises**

## Subtitle

A minimal laboratory prototype for source-grounded answers, jurisdiction limits, service boundaries, and safer update loops.

## Main idea

Most large language models are designed to answer.

Legal AI must first learn when **not** to answer.

This prototype demonstrates a minimal safety-first approach:

- no source, no legal claim;
- no verified jurisdiction, no jurisdiction-specific answer;
- no service authority, no attorney-like action;
- no update promotion without QA discipline.

---

## Why the prototype is intentionally minimal

The code in this repository is not intended to compete with full RAG frameworks, vector databases, or production AI platforms.

It is intentionally small and dependency-free so the safety logic remains easy to inspect.

The focus is not algorithmic novelty.

The focus is legal AI boundary design:

- source boundaries;
- jurisdiction boundaries;
- service boundaries;
- out-of-scope boundaries;
- unsupported-claim tracking;
- separation of safety metrics from coverage metrics;
- Active/Shadow update discipline.
